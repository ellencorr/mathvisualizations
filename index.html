Visualizing Abstract Algebra

Exploring groups, rings, and fields through popular theorems and simple visuals. Built from a love of abstract math and a curiosity for making it more tangible.

Purpose

This project is meant to complement textbooks and courses by offering visual intuition for concepts that are often abstract and difficult to picture. The goal isn’t to replace formal definitions or proofs, but to provide a way of seeing the structure behind them.

What is abstract algebra?

Abstract algebra studies structures like groups, rings, and fields, focusing on how they behave and interact rather than what they’re made of.

Group Theory

Isomorphism Theorems (Groups)

Simple visual aids for the First through Fourth Isomorphism Theorems of groups (as presented in Abstract Algebra, Third Edition by Dummit & Foote). Each theorem is accompanied by a visual and a guided outline of its proof.

First Isomorphism Theorem

Let G and H be groups. If there exists a homomorphism φ: G → H, then ker(φ) is a normal subgroup of G and G / ker(φ) ≅ Im[φ].

Second Isomorphism Theorem

Let G be a group and H a normal subgroup of G. If A is any subgroup of G, then H∩A is also a normal subgroup of G and A/(H ∩ A) ≅ HA/A.

Step-by-step visualization of the structure of the proof.

Third Isomorphism Theorem

Let G be a group and A a normal subgroup of G. Suppose H is a normal subgroup of G and A. Then A/H is a normal subgroup of G/H and (G/H)/(A/H) ≅ G/A.

Proof Outline

Step-by-step visualization of the structure of the proof.

Fourth Isomorphism Theorem

Let G be a group. If N is a normal subgroup of G then there exists a bijection between the subgroups of G containing N and the quotient group G/N.
